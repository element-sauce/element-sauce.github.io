Who Am I

Prompted Grok with "Draw an image depicting a man with a black and white checkered mask covering their entire face playing chess in watercolor style."

I am a sophomore studying Computer Science at the University of North Carolina at Charlotte. I learned the rules of chess at a young age and played chess for a few years at my primary school chess club, and then at the local community chess club. After I started learning about programming, I gradually stopped playing altogether. During the chess boom in popular culture that occurred largely during the initial pandemic period at the start of the second decade in the twenty-first century, it forced itself onto my radar again. Recently, I've been keeping up periodically with chess news, and will play a game or two when I'm not studying.

Recently, I found myself liking the idea of freestyle chess more than standard chess. Standard chess has the same predetermined starting arrangement of pieces, but freestyle chess starts at a position randomly chosen from nine hundred and sixty positions. This addresses the criticism by several top contemporary chess players and their predecessors (most notably Robert "Bobby" Fischer) about the prevalance of the preparation of sequences of moves called lines prior to the match, which may stifle creativity. At the expert levels, it is not unheard of for top players known as "grandmasters" to have the first twenty or more moves of a game made, based on memorized lines. With many more starting positions, it is infeasible for players to have prepared lines of such depth, in the variant of chess known as freestyle.

When to play chess?

Two Men Playing Chess - autochrome by Alfred Stieglitz, CC0

Chess is a versatile game that can easily be played outdoors during pleasant weather, offering an enjoyable experience in natural surroundings. During the day, on a sunny day or in mild weather, players can set up a chessboard in parks, gardens, or outdoor cafés, enjoying the fresh air and the relaxing ambiance. The game can be a perfect social activity, allowing friends and family to gather outdoors, while also providing a stimulating challenge. Playing chess outside can be especially rewarding, as it brings a combination of strategic thinking and the calming effects of nature.

On the other hand, chess can also be played indoors, making it a timeless activity that can be enjoyed regardless of the weather or time of day. Whether it's raining, snowing, or dark outside, the game provides an opportunity for mental engagement in a comfortable indoor setting. Indoors, players can focus without distraction, and the stability of the environment ensures that the game can be played at any time, whether during the day or late into the night. Whether you're looking for casual matches, ranked games, or to challenge top players, online platforms provide endless opportunities to engage with the global chess community at any time.

Learning to play chess begins with understanding its basic rules, which are readily accessible through a variety of resources. Introductory books, Wikipedia articles, or online tutorials are excellent places to start, as they provide a comprehensive breakdown of how each piece moves, the objective of the game, and special rules like castling, en passant, and pawn promotion. Many of these resources also include diagrams and examples that make grasping the fundamentals relatively easy. For beginners, this initial learning phase is quick and straightforward, and it provides the foundation necessary to begin playing the game.

However, the true learning in chess comes from consistent practice and experience, and it’s in playing actual games where improvement happens. Simply memorizing the rules is not enough to become proficient; one must engage with the game regularly to internalize strategy, tactics, and pattern recognition. As you play, it's crucial to notate your moves, either manually or using digital tools, so that you can review and reflect on your games afterward. By analyzing your games, especially the ones you lose, you can identify mistakes and missed opportunities, learning valuable lessons from each game. Chess is a skill that deepens with repetition, reflection, and the continuous application of knowledge gained through play, making practice the key to truly mastering the game.

Why should you play chess?

Prompted Grok with "Draw an image depicting a man thinking pensively during a chess game in watercolor style."

Playing chess offers significant intellectual benefits, providing a powerful form of mental exercise that enhances cognitive abilities. The game challenges players to think critically, plan ahead, and develop problem-solving skills, all of which help strengthen memory and concentration. Chess requires players to evaluate multiple possible moves and anticipate their opponent’s strategies, promoting strategic thinking and fostering the ability to think several steps ahead. Additionally, regular chess play can improve decision-making skills, as players must weigh the consequences of their choices and adapt to an ever-changing board. These intellectual benefits extend beyond the chessboard, as the cognitive skills honed through playing chess can enhance other aspects of life, from academic performance to professional tasks requiring analysis and foresight.

Beyond its intellectual advantages, chess also offers a profound lesson in self-accountability. In chess, every game you lose is entirely your fault, as the game is governed by clear, logical rules that leave no room for chance. With perfect play from both sides, a game of chess will always end in a draw or tie, meaning that any defeat is a direct result of your own decisions and mistakes. This harsh reality forces players to take full responsibility for their actions on the board. There is no one to blame but yourself for missed opportunities, poor strategies, or tactical errors. This teaches the invaluable lesson of self-reflection, as each loss becomes an opportunity to critically examine your choices and learn from them. In a world full of external factors, chess offers a rare space where outcomes are purely shaped by your own skill, decisions, and improvement, emphasizing total personal accountability in a way few other activities can.

What is chess?

Image: Chess game Staunton No. 6 by Wilfredor, CC0

Chess is a timeless and intellectually stimulating board game played between two opponents on a square 8x8 grid. Each player begins with 16 pieces: one king, one queen, two rooks, two knights, two bishops, and eight pawns, all of which move in distinct ways. The primary objective of the game is to checkmate the opponent's king, meaning to place the king in a position where it is under attack and cannot escape capture. Chess combines both strategy and tactics, requiring players to think ahead, plan several moves in advance, and adapt to an evolving game. Its depth and complexity have earned it a reputation as one of the greatest games of skill in the world, with millions of enthusiasts globally playing it casually and competitively, both online and in person.

Image: Das Schachzabelbuch held and digitised by the British Library, CC0

The history of chess spans over a thousand years, with its origins tracing back to ancient India around the 6th century AD. The precursor to modern chess, known as chaturanga, was a strategy game that closely resembled chess, featuring similar pieces and moves, and was played on an 8x8 grid. From India, the game spread to Persia, where it became known as shatranj. The game was further refined and popularized in the Islamic world during the 7th and 8th centuries. By the 10th century, chess reached Europe through Spain, where it became especially popular in the courts of kings and nobles. During the Middle Ages, the rules of the game began to change, and by the 15th century, major rule modifications, such as the powerful moves of the queen and bishop, shaped the game into the version we recognize today. The 19th century saw the establishment of official competitive play, leading to the creation of the first World Chess Championship in 1886. Chess continues to evolve, with modern technology and the rise of online platforms bringing the game to a new generation of players worldwide.
